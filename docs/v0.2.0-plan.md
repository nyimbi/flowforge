## 0. What changed from v0

Tier-A (5 strategic verticals) gets named human-SME signoff at audit-2026 quality bar. 4. **All 8 audit-2026 invariants gated per X3 PR** (must-fix #9). v0's "invariants 2/3/4" is replaced by the full P0+P1 set per close-out §"Architecture invariants".
5. **Per-ticket DELIBERATE signoff rows** (must-fix #10). Every E-75..E-84 row carries the audit-2026 close-out schema (`evidence`, `acceptance_tests`, `pre_deploy_checks`, `post_deploy_checks`, `atomic_fix_proof`, per-finding `f-mitigation`).
6. **7 cross-domain registries as pre-X2 deliverables** (must-fix #15). Citations, channels, roles, doc-types, JTBD IDs, edge-case IDs, SLA-key conventions — authored before any Tier-B content drafting begins.
7. **All 5 §13 questions ANSWERED** (must-fix #17). Architect §6.5–§6.9 answers integrated into §3 (release shape), §4 (per-tier counts), §5 (forks_enabled flag shape), §6 (first vertical = insurance), §11 (cadence).
8. **8-scenario pre-mortem** (must-fix #4). v0's 5 scenarios + F-6 (signing-kms boot crash), F-7 (workspace flip churn), F-8 (revocation propagation window).
9. **Critical-path math restated** (must-fix #20). Engineering critical path = **6 wk** for v0.2.0 (not 10); content rolls through v0.2.x without gating engine semver.
10. **Per-workflow `engine_features` declaration** (must-fix #8). Ships with the global `forks_enabled` flag, not as a v0.3.0 retrofit.

## 1. RALPLAN-DR Summary

### 1.1 Six guiding principles

| # | Principle | Why it matters here |
|---|---|---|
| **P-1** | **Reuse the audit-2026 quality template for every Tier-A JTBD.** | The lint bar (data_capture ≥3, edge_cases ≥1, documents_required ≥1, approvals ≥1, notifications ≥1, sla, data_sensitivity, compliance, signoff) is non-negotiable for Tier-A. Tier-B passes the same lint but ships under a weaker `reviewer_credentials` label. |
| **P-2** | **AI-author then domain-SME validate — with named human SMEs for Tier-A.** | v0 permitted "the same Claude agent acting in domain-SME role with citation discipline". v1 strikes that. Tier-A requires named human SMEs (audit-2026 S2b precedent: 5 SMEs × 5 wk for 30 JTBDs). Tier-B explicitly does not — and is labelled accordingly so consumers know. |
| **P-3** | **Parallelize across independent workers; coordinate on three frozen schemas + seven cross-domain registries.** | The schema lock distinguishes (a) JTBD content schema (frozen during X2 — `flowforge_jtbd.dsl.spec.JtbdLintSpec`), (b) audit-event canonical body (frozen for v0.2.0 — invariant 7), (c) audit-event payload metadata (additive-only, see E-73 R-3). Seven cross-domain registries (citations, channels, roles, doc-types, JTBD IDs, edge-case IDs, SLA-keys) ship as pre-X2 deliverables before any Tier-B drafting starts. |
| **P-4** | **Engine wiring (E-74) ships behind a layered feature flag; never regress any audit-2026 conformance invariant.** | Layered = global `flowforge.engine.forks_enabled` (default-off in v0.2.0; default-on in v0.3.0 only after a v0.2.x soak window with documented opt-in production data) AND per-workflow manifest declaration `engine_features: [parallel_fork]`. Both must align before fork dispatches. All 8 invariants (P0: 1/2/3/7; P1: 4/5/6/8) are required-green per X3 PR — no exceptions. |
| **P-5** | **Every deliverable lands as a PR with conformance suite green AND DELIBERATE signoff row populated.** | No "land then test", no "land then sign". Each engineering ticket and each domain JTBD lands with its acceptance test + per-ticket signoff schema row in `framework/docs/v0.2.0/signoff-checklist.md`. The audit-2026 CI gate (`scripts/ci/check_signoff.py`) is extended to v0.2.0 tickets. |
| **P-6** | **Releases are staged. Engineering ships at v0.2.0; content rolls through v0.2.x; full coverage targets v0.3.0.** | **NEW IN V1.** Decouples SME signoff throughput (the bottleneck) from engine release cadence. Per-domain `flowforge-jtbd-<domain>` packages flip `package=false → package=true` independently as content + SME signoff lands. Mirrors the audit-2026 F-5 mitigation (two-step rollout) that v0 inadvertently abandoned. |

### 1.2 Top three decision drivers

| # | Driver | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **D-1** | **Engineering follow-ups have a hard scope ceiling; content has none.** | E-73 phases 4–6, E-75 emitters, E-76 JWT, E-78b Instance.tokens, E-79..E-82 fork wiring = 8 engineering tickets, ~6 wk on a single engineering critical path. Content scope is "30 domains × 30 JTBDs = 900" but with no upper bound and SME-throughput dependent. **Don't couple them.** v0.2.0 ships engineering; v0.2.x ships content as it earns signoff. |
| **D-2** | **Security follow-ups (E-73 phases 4–6 + real JWT extractor + per-fix metric emitters) require DELIBERATE-mode signoff.** | Per-user audit identity, JWT verification, and metric emission all touch security boundaries. They get signoff-checklist rows + observability validation just like audit-2026 P0/P1 fixes. |
| **D-3** | **E-74 phases 1–5 wires `parallel_fork` through `fire()`; this is high-risk to invariants 1–8 (all P0+P1).** | `engine/fire.py` carries the entire audit-2026 hardening (E-32 EPIC + E-39 + E-40). Touching it without the layered feature flag risks regressing any of the 8 invariants. Mitigation: layered flag default-off, per-PR full-invariant gate, single-EPIC-PR convention, CODEOWNERS lock, 24h soak hard gate before flag flip. |

### 1.3 Three viable options with pros/cons

| Option | Description | Pros | Cons | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Sequential by capability** | Engineering tracks (X1: metrics + JWT + E-73) → Content track (X2: 875 JTBDs) → Engine wiring (X3: E-74). One thing at a time. | Simpler coordination; content team picks up after schema is stable. | Total elapsed time ~12 weeks; blocks content track for ~2 weeks while engineering finishes X1; couples engine release to SME throughput. | **Rejected.** |
| **B — Monolithic v0.2.0 (v0 plan, "parallel-with-shared-schema-lock")** | X1 engineering + X2 content + X3 engine wiring all ship in one v0.2.0 GA, ~10 wk. Schema frozen at sprint kick-off. | Matches user's "by teams in parallel" guidance for content; one announcement; one PyPI cut. | Couples SME signoff pace to engine release; re-introduces audit-2026 F-5 risk by demanding all 30 pkgs flip `package=true` in one window; "30 SMEs" = 1 Claude agent in 30 personas without named-human review (architect V-1 BLOCKER). | **Rejected** (architect V-1 + T-1). |
| **C — Staged release: engineering-only v0.2.0 + rolling-content v0.2.x (CHOSEN)** | v0.2.0 (wk 6) = engine + identity + metrics. v0.2.1..v0.2.6 (wk 8–20) = per-batch content drops, each gated on per-domain SME signoff (Tier-A) or AI-authored citation-anchored disclosure (Tier-B). v0.3.0 (Q3 2026) = full Tier-A coverage + flag-flip. | Decouples engineering from SME throughput; per-domain `package=false → package=true` flip inherits audit-2026 F-5 mitigation; Tier-A quality bar preserved; Tier-B labelling honest about AI authorship; engineering ships in 6 wk; content shipping curve matches realistic SME availability. | Two-class catalogue (Tier-A vs Tier-B) requires consumer-facing labelling; cross-domain registries become hard pre-X2 dependencies; more semver coordination across the v0.2.x series. | **Chosen** (architect §6.5 + T-1 synthesis). |

**Choice**: Option C. Driven by P-6 (staged releases), P-2 (named SMEs for Tier-A), D-1 (decouple eng from content), D-3 (engine risk concentrates in week 6 — a 6-wk focused sprint matches it).

| # | v0 question | v1 answer | Source |

| **Q-5** | Content-only PyPI sub-release ahead of engineering, or all-or-nothing? | **Staged.** v0.2.0 (wk 6) = engineering only. v0.2.1 (wk 8) = first 5 verticals top-up to ≥15 JTBDs each (Tier-A). v0.2.2..v0.2.6 (wk 10–20) = rolling 5-domain Tier-B batches. v0.3.0 (Q3 2026) = full Tier-A coverage + `forks_enabled` default-on + deprecation bridges retired. | architect §6.5 + §6.8, must-fix #5 + #13 |

## 12. ADR — Architecture Decision Record

### 12.1 Decision

Adopt **Option C (staged release: engineering-only v0.2.0 + rolling-content v0.2.x)** to ship FlowForge engineering in **6 calendar weeks** and content as it earns SME signoff across **v0.2.1..v0.2.7 over weeks 6–20**, with full Tier-A coverage targeted at **v0.3.0 (Q3 2026)**.

### 12.2 Drivers

1. **Engineering and content have different cadences** (D-1): engineering is bounded-scope, ~6 wk on a single critical path; content is SME-throughput-bound. Coupling them (v0 plan Option B) causes either content rush (quality drops) or engine delay (security follow-ups stranded).
2. **Security follow-ups need DELIBERATE signoff** (D-2): E-73 phases 4–6, E-76 JWT, E-75 metric emitters all ship in v0.2.0 X1.
3. **Engine wiring is high-risk** (D-3): E-78b + E-79..E-82 must not regress any of 8 invariants; layered feature flag + 24h soak hard gate.

### 12.3 Alternatives considered

| Option | Reason for rejection |
|---|---|
| A — Sequential by capability | ~12 wk total; blocks content for 2 wk; misses user's "parallel teams" guidance. |
| B — Monolithic v0.2.0 (v0 plan) | Couples SME signoff to engine release; "30 SMEs" without named humans; abandons audit-2026 F-5 two-step rollout; architect V-1 BLOCKER. |

### 12.4 Why C chosen

- Decouples engineering release cadence from SME availability.
- Per-domain `package=true` flips inherit audit-2026 F-5 mitigation discipline.
- Tier-A quality bar preserved (named human SME per audit-2026 S2b precedent).
- Tier-B labelling is honest about AI authorship (machine-readable + README disclaimer).
- Engineering ships in 6 wk on a single critical path (must-fix #20).
- Content shipping curve matches realistic SME throughput (audit-2026: 5 SMEs × 5 wk → 30 JTBDs).

### 12.5 Consequences

**Positive**:
- v0.2.0 ships in 6 wk with engineering follow-ups closed and zero invariant regressions.
- v0.2.x rolling content earns signoff per-domain rather than en masse.
- v0.3.0 milestone is well-scoped (full Tier-A coverage + flag-flip + Tier-B promotions).
- Engine wiring lands behind layered feature flag — zero regression risk to v0.1.x deployments.

**Negative**:
- Two-class catalogue (Tier-A vs Tier-B) requires consumer-facing labelling discipline.
- Cross-domain registries (R-01..R-07) become hard pre-X2 dependencies.
- More semver coordination across the v0.2.x series.

**Mitigations**: All eight risks (R-1..R-8) have named owners + concrete actions + rollback paths.

## Citation accuracy

This package's JTBD content was AI-authored against the FlowForge
cross-domain citation registry (`flowforge_jtbd.registries.citations`)

been validated by a named human domain SME.

  may not reflect the most recent statutory updates. Confirm against
  current law before operational use.

  substitute for domain-expert review.

(no `-starter-extended` suffix) where available.
